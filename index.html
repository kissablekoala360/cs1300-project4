Relay: Facilitated Peer Support

A case study in iterative design.

INTRO: Relay

Our goal for this project was to design an interactive interface for a startup. We created and received feedback on muliple iterations of our interfaces.

What is Relay?

Relay is a startup that wants to help people overcome addition through facilitated connection and shame-free accountability.

For example, Relay connects 3-7 people struggling with alcohol addiction, making it easy for the vulnerable to reach out and create positive change in their lives.

Prototype-1

Let's take a look at our first interactive prototype using our final wireframe.

Peer Critique

Next, we attended a critique session with an industry guest and peers. We received diverse feedback on our prototype to help us better polish our final product.

Here's a summary of what was said and how we altered our prototype in response:

Navbar: Placement + Interaction States

+

The Critiques

  • Navbar position is inconsistent when changing pages
  • Users were unclear on which navbar option they were currently visiting

Our Changes

  • Gave navbar a fixed position on every page
  • Underlined the navbar option that corresponds to their current page


Changing States: Inconsistencies

+

The Critiques

  • Navbar is significantly different when logged in vs logged out (Home -> Dashboard ...etc)
  • Get Started button on Home page links to login page which is misleading


Our Changes

  • Removed nav bar when logged in and added a hamburger menu button
  • Added a sign up page which Get Started now links to

Color Contrast + Other

+

The Critiques

  • The pink on white theme was difficult to read
  • Empty text bubbles could be misleading
  • Prototype does not demonstrate full use cases
  • Image on front page implies that Relay is a dating app
  • No need for marketing pages when logged in

Our Changes

  • Added drop shadow to text for readability
  • Added filler text to bubbles
  • Added interaction for multiple addictions and chat sending feature
  • Removed heart from image on front page
  • Removed About Us page from nav bar when logged in

Prototype-2

Based on the above critiques and fixes, we created a second prototype to test with users.

User Tests

We submitted our final prototype to UserTesting.com, where real users completed a set of interaction steps based on our instructions. We also provided some context to the users about Relay.

The Steps:

You are a frequent user of Relay who struggles with porn and alcohol addictions. Today, you want to send a message to your alcohol addiction peers through the Relay desktop website.


  • Log into your existing Relay account
  • Switch from your porn addiction dashboard to your alcohol addiction dashboard
  • Send a message to your alcohol addiction peers through the chat room
  • Log out of your account

These steps tested a diverse range of functionality on our prototype using a real life scenario.


The Results:

In general, the three users found our site to be intuitive and praised our use of avatars to gamify the platform.

They gave 5 star ratings on all the tasks except for two 4 star ratings given by user 1 for navigating between dashboards and signing out. She commented that we should label the dashboard toggle button and place the sign out button in the dashboard page so it is easier to locate.
